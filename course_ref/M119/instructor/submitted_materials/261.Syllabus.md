# Syllabus

## Course Description

*Offering:* Full Semester
   
*Pre-Requisite:* (CS 101, CSE 110 or CS 124, CIT 160) and (ACT 23 or SAT 540 (Post 2016, 570)) or ALEKS 55 or MATH 101 or MATH110X or MATH109 or FDMAT112)

## Course Outcomes

By the end of the semester, each student will be able to:

1. Apply their understanding of functions across discrete and continuous probability distributions and algebraic functions as mathematical models.
 1. Communicate mathematics using correct mathematical notation and appreciate the power of mathematical notation's role in data analytics.
 1. Use symbolic and numerical methods of differentiation to optimize functions and find the linear approximation of functions.
 1. Use symbolic and numerical methods of integration to calculate probabilities using probability density functions and cumulative distribution functions and derive expected value and variance of functions of random variables.

# Course Details

## Overview

*How does this course relate to other courses?*

This course builds a foundation of mathematics to support learning in MATH 325, MATH 326, MATH 425, and CSE 450. It will also solidify the probability  and statistical summary concepts introduced in MATH 221.

*What do I need to be able to do before taking this course?*

Students need to have a comfortable understanding of algebraic manipulation.

*What will I be prepared to do after this course?*

Students will be able to use the principles associated with calculus to understand the mathematics behind statistical summaries, statistical distributions, and elements of machine learning.

## Semester Outline

Below is a 14-week outline of the material we will cover.


- **Week 1 & 2:** Functions and Notation: Establish a common language about functions and common notation. Work with polynomials, logarithm and exponential functions, indexing, finite sums, notation of infinite sums, 2D function (both scalar valued and vector valued), independent variables/parameters/dependent variables, discrete vs. continuous functions, piecewise functions, etc. 
- **Week 3 & 4:** Derivatives and Optimization 
- **Week 5 - 7:** Derivatives 2: Linear Approximation, Higher Order derivatives, Derivatives and Optimization in higher dimensions 
- **Week 8:** Examples of Infinite series in mathematics for analytics and numerical calculations (Discrete probability distributions, Taylor polynomials, Approximations using Taylor polynomials) 
- **Week 9-11:** Integration and Probability: Relationship between probability distributions functions to cumulative distribution functions (Fundamental Theorem of Calculus), using integration to calculate probabilities, Expected values and Variance. Riemann sums and approximate integration. 
- **Week 12 -14:** Multivariate integration and readings on mathematical models in data science and analytics and presentations on math in analytics.
