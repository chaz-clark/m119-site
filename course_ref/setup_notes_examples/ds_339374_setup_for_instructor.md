## General Setup for Instructor

1. Complete the Instructor Pre-Semester Checklist that Online Learning emailed to you.
   1. You will receive this email shortly before the semester begins.
   2. If you feel you should have received the email, but have not yet received it, please contact the [Instructor Support Line](https://content.byui.edu/integ/gen/8872d2b2-91d5-4953-a357-3097ef2aa5d0/0/?.vi=file&attachment.uuid=8a632af8-a545-4b4a-b63a-7ac9c2648b21).
2. Ensure that your Instructor Profile is updated with your office hours, Zoom URL, and other information.

## Course Specific Setup for Instructor

### Sample Email

Please send a welcome email to your students as soon as you have access to your class roll (likely the weekend before the semester begins) to establish expectations and your desired learning environment. Your email should be welcoming, inviting, and informative. Please include the following in your email:

* Explain when the class will start and how to get started in the course.

+ Invite students to access the course promptly.
+ Inform students they will need to complete assignments according to the set due dates.
+ Let students know how much time they will likely need to focus toward the course each week. The average is 3–4 hours each week per credit in a 14-week semester, or double that if the class runs for a 7-week block.
+ Encourage students to install the Chrome plugin; it helps us gather **non-personal** data about their experience to improve courses. They should follow the [Setup Instructions](https://content.byui.edu/integ/gen/b6e4e7a3-2037-43eb-9316-663cb7e02878/0/RealUserMonitoring_ChromeExtensionInstructions.pdf) to install the plugin.

* Explain any unique course characteristics.
* Explain how students can contact you.

+ Provide your email address and, **if** you would like, a phone number.
+ **If** you hold office hours, provide details on when and how to access your virtual office. Be specific with your time zone.

* Remind students to use appropriate etiquette and respect in their interactions with you and their classmates.
* Personalize your welcome email with additional information as desired.

 

**Sample Email Template**

Dear students, 

Welcome to ***[course name]***! My name is ***[name]***, and I will be your instructor. I am looking forward to getting to work with you as we ***[vision for this subject/personal note]***. 

The semester will begin on [date], but you are invited to explore the course as soon as you have access to familiarize yourself with it. Before you get started in the course, please take time to review [BYU-Idaho University Policies](https://content.byui.edu/file/d24f576f-d34b-47be-a466-d00bd4792fb6/29/universitypolicies.html). 

Access the course by logging into [I-Learn](https://byui.instructure.com/) and selecting the course code on your dashboard. This is a ***[#]***-credit, ***[#]***-week class. Most students who succeed in this class spend an average of ***[#]*** hours on course work each week. 

Start by reading the syllabus, which will give you information about the course and upcoming assignments, then get started on the first assignment. You will need to follow the assignment due dates. 

I strongly encourage you to communicate with me, as I am available to help answer questions and concerns. You can visit with me during my office hour on ***[day]*** at ***[time and time zone]*** by visiting my virtual office link, which can be found in the course Overview page. If you can’t attend my office hour, you can email me your questions at ***[email@byui.edu]*** or call me at ***[123-123-1234] [NOTE: you are not required to provide a telephone number]***. I strive to reply within 24 hours.

If you would like to discuss a graded assignment, review the feedback provided before contacting me. To find feedback on your assignment, please review the article titled [How Do I Find Assignment Feedback?](https://byu-idaho.screenstepslive.com/s/16998/m/65436/l/1215037-how-do-i-find-assignment-feedback-copied?token=qFM5FwQ58hAh15jjX9U5WF2v0MXTI2-m) 

I am looking forward to our time together as a class!

Sincerely, 

[Name]
